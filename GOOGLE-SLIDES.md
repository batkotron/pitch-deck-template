# Google Slides Template

A free, copyable Google Slides template that matches the structure in [`SLIDES.md`](./SLIDES.md).

## How to use it

1. Open the template link below.
2. Click **File > Make a copy** to create your own editable version.
3. Replace the placeholder content with your own, using [`SLIDES.md`](./SLIDES.md) as a guide.
4. Run through [`CHECKLIST.md`](./CHECKLIST.md) before sending.

## Design principles

If you're building your own slides from scratch, follow these:

- **One idea per slide.** If you have two ideas, you have two slides.
- **High contrast.** Dark text on light, or light text on dark. Never grey on grey.
- **Big type.** 24pt minimum for body text. Headlines 36pt+.
- **No more than 30 words per slide.** If you can't say it in 30 words, you don't understand it yet.
- **Real screenshots, not mockups.** Mockups signal "not built yet."
- **One typeface, two weights.** Inter, Helvetica, or your brand font. Don't mix three fonts.

## Tools to consider

- **Google Slides** - free, easy to share, easy to copy. Best default.
- **Pitch.com** - clean templates, version control, collaborator-friendly.
- **Figma** - best for design control, worst for non-designers.
- **Keynote** - beautiful out of the box, harder to share.

## What NOT to use

- **PowerPoint with stock templates.** Investors have seen all of them.
- **Canva templates.** They look generic at this point.
- **PDF-only decks.** Make a Google Slides version too - investors share, and a shareable link converts better than an attachment.
